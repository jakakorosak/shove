Overview

Shove is a two-player strategy game played on a square grid. Players take turns placing and pushing slabs, trying to achieve one of three winning conditions. The rules are few; the strategy runs deep.

Components

  • A square grid board (default 6×6, configurable up to 12×12)
  • Two players: White and Black
  • Each player begins with a reserve of slabs (default 18), all off the board

Turn Structure

White moves first. Both steps are mandatory every turn. If either cannot be completed, you lose immediately.

Step 1 — Place

Take one slab from your reserve and place it on any empty square. You may not place on a square orthogonally adjacent to any opponent slab.

Step 2 — Move

Select one of your slabs and slide it one square orthogonally (up, down, left, right — no diagonals). If the destination is occupied, a push occurs.

Pushing

A push moves the entire contiguous line of slabs in the direction of travel by one square. It is legal only if the last slab has room (empty square or off the edge) and your pushing power meets or exceeds the opponent slabs in the line.

Pushing power

  • The moving slab itself
  • All your slabs anywhere in the line ahead (non-contiguous counts)
  • All your slabs contiguously behind the moving slab

Your own slabs in the line offer no resistance.

w w b b → 2 pushers vs 2 blacks → legal w b w b → 2 pushers vs 2 blacks → legal w b b b → 1 pusher vs 3 blacks → illegal

Removal

Pushed off the edge

Any slab pushed off the board returns to its owner's reserve and may be placed again.

Isolation

After every move, any slab that had at least one orthogonal neighbour before the move but has none after is removed and returned to reserve. Slabs already isolated before the move are unaffected.

A player may deliberately isolate their own slab to return it to reserve — useful for freeing placement squares or escaping worse outcomes.

Winning

Win conditions are checked immediately after the move step:

1No legal turn. Your opponent cannot place (reserve empty or all squares adjacent to your slabs) or cannot move or push any slab. Either alone is a loss.
2Board clear. After a push, your opponent has zero slabs on the board. Reserve does not matter.
3Connection. After a push, your slabs form a single orthogonally connected region covering at least half the squares of the board.

Notation

Each turn is two mandatory tokens separated by a space. Coordinates are written as col,row, both numbered from 1.

  • Placement: 3,4
  • Move or push: 3,4-3,5

White and black turns share a numbered line, separated by |:

1. 3,4 2,4-2,5 | 5,6 4,6-4,5 2. 1,2 3,2-3,3 | 6,6 5,6-5,5

Clarifications

  • Both steps are mandatory. Inability to complete either is an immediate loss.
  • Orthogonal means up, down, left, right only. Diagonals count for nothing.
  • The placement restriction applies only during placement. Movement may bring slabs into contact freely.
  • Slabs always return to reserve when removed. They may be placed again.
  • Win conditions 2 and 3 are triggered only by a push, not a simple move to an empty square.

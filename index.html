Overview

Shove is a two-player strategy game played on a square grid. Players take turns placing and pushing slabs, trying to achieve one of three winning conditions. The rules are few; the strategy runs deep.

Components

  • A square grid board (default 6×6, configurable up to 12×12)
  • Two players: White and Black
  • Each player begins with a reserve of slabs (default 18), all off the board

Turn Structure

White moves first. Both steps are mandatory every turn. If either cannot be completed, you lose immediately.

Step 1 — Place

Take a slab from your reserve and place it on any empty square not orthogonally adjacent to an opponent slab.

Step 2 — Move

Slide one of your slabs one square orthogonally. If the destination is occupied, a push occurs.

White places, then moves one square right

Pushing

A push moves the entire contiguous line of slabs one square in the direction of travel. It is legal if the last slab has room and your pushing power meets or exceeds the opponent slabs in the line.

Pushing power

  • The moving slab itself
  • All your slabs anywhere in the line ahead (non-contiguous counts)
  • All your slabs contiguously behind the moving slab

Your own slabs in the line offer no resistance.

W W B B — 2 whites push 2 blacks

Removal

Pushed off the edge

Any slab pushed off the board returns to its owner's reserve and may be placed again.

Black slab pushed off the edge → reserve

Isolation

After every move, any slab that had a neighbour before the move but has none after is returned to reserve. A player may deliberately isolate their own slab as a tactical choice.

White moves away — black slab becomes isolated and is removed

Winning

Win conditions are checked after placement and after every move:

1No legal turn. Opponent cannot place or cannot move any slab.
2Board clear. After a push, opponent has zero slabs on the board.
3Connection. Your slabs form a single connected region covering at least half the board.
White achieves a connected region covering half the board

Notation

Each turn is two tokens separated by a space. Coordinates are col,row from 1. White and black share a line separated by |:

1. 3,4 2,4-2,5 | 5,6 4,6-4,5 2. 1,2 3,2-3,3 | 6,6 5,6-5,5

Clarifications

  • Both steps are mandatory. Inability to complete either is an immediate loss.
  • Orthogonal means up, down, left, right only. Diagonals count for nothing.
  • The placement restriction applies only during placement. Movement may bring slabs into contact freely.
  • Slabs always return to reserve when removed. They may be placed again.
